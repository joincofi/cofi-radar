Process

How the index works

Four steps, repeated weekly. Designed to resist noise and reward consensus.
01

Panel input

A curated panel of ~100 senior product, design, and engineering leaders ("The Benchmark 100") answers the same question weekly:

"If a credible product launched today without this capability, would it be perceived as behind?"

02

Three-state aggregation

Responses are aggregated into three observable states:

Not Expected Optional Industry Standard
03

Conservative movement rules

Upgrades require sustained agreement. Downgrades require prolonged confirmation. Most weeks show no change by design.

04

Weekly delivery

Customers receive a weekly email and dashboard update showing current status and direction of expectation (Increasing or Stable).

Signals

Expectation direction

Each tracked capability includes a direction indicator. This reflects the trend in panel sentiment, not a forecast.

↗ Increasing

Expectation pressure is building. Teams should prepare for a potential status change and assess readiness.

→ Stable

No meaningful change in panel sentiment. Current classification reflects ongoing consensus.

CoFi provides early warning when obligations are rising. It does not speculate on when relief might come.
